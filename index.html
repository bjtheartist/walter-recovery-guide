HOME RECOVERY GUIDE
For Walter

The Daily
Work

A home exercise companion for rebuilding hip strength, balance, and confidence. One rep at a time. One step, not one day.
TrainerBilly Ndizeye, NASM-CPT FocusRight-side hip flexor recovery FrequencyDaily, 15–25 min
A Note From Billy

Walter, think of your hip flexor like an old circuit. The switch works. The bulb works. The wire between them is frayed. Every time you lift your knee, with or without your arm helping, you are repairing that wire.

The goal is not to load more weight. The goal is to fire the muscle with intention, slowly, and more times than yesterday. Quality of attention matters more than speed. Your brain is rebuilding the pathway, and it pays attention to what you pay attention to.

Show up small and often. That beats showing up big and rarely, every time.

— Billy
Before You Begin
  • Keep your cane or a sturdy chair within reach at all times
  • Have a family member nearby for any exercise done standing
  • Stop if you feel dizzy, lightheaded, or short of breath. Rest and try again later
  • Go slow. Three seconds up. Three seconds down. Slow is the point, not the problem
  • If something hurts sharply, stop. Dull muscle effort is good, sharp pain is not
SECTION ONE

Warm-Up

Five minutes. Seated. Every session begins here.
Seated · Daily

Gentle Activation

Priming the circuit. Blood flow first, strength second.
01Ankle Pumps & Circles
15 each · both feet
Lift one foot slightly off the floor. Point your toes down, then pull them up toward your shin. Then draw slow circles clockwise, then counterclockwise.
CueWake up the circulation from the ground up.
02Seated Knee Extensions
2 × 10 · each leg
Sit tall in a sturdy chair, feet flat. Slowly straighten one knee until the leg is almost straight. Hold for three seconds. Lower slowly over three seconds.
CueThe lowering matters as much as the lifting. Don't let gravity do the work.
Watch at Flint Rehab
03Seated Marching
2 × 10 · each leg
Sit tall. Lift your knee toward your chest as high as you can. Use your arm to help if you need to. Set it down with control.
CueLift from the hip, not from leaning back. Keep the chest tall.
Watch at Flint Rehab
SECTION TWO

Foundation Work

Glutes, hamstrings, and core. The platform everything else stands on.
Supine & Side-Lying · Daily

Floor & Bed Exercises

These can all be done on a bed. No floor transfer needed.
04Glute Bridges
2 × 10 · 3 sec hold
Lying on your back, knees bent, feet flat on the bed. Squeeze your glutes and lift your hips off the bed. Hold three seconds at the top. Lower slowly.
CueThink about pressing your heels into the bed, not lifting your hips.
Watch at Flint Rehab
05Heel Slides
2 × 10 · each leg
Lying on your back, legs straight. Slide one heel toward your butt, bending the knee. Slide it back out straight. Keep the heel in contact with the bed the entire time.
CueSlow and connected. No popping or jerking.
06Supine Hip Abduction
2 × 10 · each leg
Lying on your back, legs straight. Slide one leg out to the side along the bed, keeping toes pointed up. Bring it back to center.
CueMove from the hip, not from leaning. Keep both hips flat.
07Clamshells (unaffected side down)
2 × 15
Lie on your left (unaffected) side with knees bent and stacked. Keep feet together. Open your top knee like a clamshell opening. Close it slowly.
CueThis fires the glute med, the steady hand of the hip.
Watch at Flint Rehab
08Straight Leg Raises
2 × 10 · each leg
Lying on your back. Bend your unaffected leg with foot flat. Keep your affected leg straight. Lift the straight leg six inches off the bed. Hold two seconds. Lower slowly.
CueIf you can't lift it unassisted yet, that's fine. Use your hand under the thigh to help, then hold the top alone.
SECTION THREE

The Hip Flexor Ladder

Five rungs. Start where you are. Move up when you can do 2 sets of 10 at the current rung with good form.
The ladder is the heart of your program. Right now you are on Rung 2 or 3. Each rung is a level of assistance you can remove as your hip gets stronger.
1
Gravity-Eliminated Motion
Side-lying on your left side with your right leg on top, knee bent, resting on a pillow. Slide the knee forward, then back. Gravity is removed from the equation.
Goal: 2 × 10 unassisted
2
Isometric Hold
Seated. Use your arm to lift your knee to the top of its range. Hold that position for three to five seconds without arm support. Lower slowly.
Goal: Hold 5 seconds × 6 reps
3
Slow Eccentric
Seated. Arm lifts your knee to the top. Remove the arm and lower the leg over a slow five-count, no arm assistance on the way down.
Goal: 2 × 6 controlled lowers
4
Reduced Assist
Loop a towel under your thigh, hold both ends. The hip must initiate the lift before the towel takes over. Over time, use less arm pull and more hip drive.
Goal: 2 × 10 with minimal towel pull
5
Unassisted Seated March
Seated. Lift the knee toward your chest with no hands, no towel, no assist. Hold briefly at the top. Lower with control.
Goal: 2 × 10 clean. This is the destination.
SECTION FOUR

Standing Work

When balance allows, and always with counter or chair for support.
Supported · When Ready

Standing Exercises

Add these when your trainer approves. Both hands on a sturdy counter. Family member present.
09Mini Sit-to-Stands
2 × 8 · elevated surface
Sit on a tall surface like the couch arm or a high stool. Stand up using your legs, hands on counter if needed. Sit back down with control over three seconds.
CueDrive through the heels. Lower slower than you rose.
10Standing Hip Abduction
2 × 10 · each leg
Holding a sturdy counter with both hands. Stand on your unaffected leg. Slowly move your affected leg out to the side. Return to center.
CueKeep your torso tall. Don't lean sideways.
11Lateral Weight Shifts
2 × 10
Stand holding the counter. Shift your weight slowly onto your affected leg. Feel the pressure under that foot. Shift back to center, then to the other side.
CueBalance is trained by shifting weight, not by standing still.
12Heel Raises
2 × 12
Stand holding the counter. Slowly rise onto the balls of both feet. Hold two seconds at the top. Lower slowly.
CueThe calves are part of the hip story. Strong calves make every step easier.
SECTION FIVE

Principles That Carry You

Four ideas to hold in mind on days when the work feels heavy.
Principle 01

Small and often beats big and rare.

Fifteen minutes of focused work every day builds more than an hour once a week. The brain rewires by repetition, not by single heroic efforts.

Principle 02

Attention builds the wire.

Before each rep, picture the movement. Between sets, rest your eyes and mentally rehearse. Neuroplasticity rewards where attention goes.

Principle 03

Both sides matter.

Train the strong side every session. The intact pathway teaches the weaker one. This is science, not politeness.

Principle 04

Progress is a ladder, not a jump.

Do not try to skip rungs. A rung earned stays earned. A rung rushed has to be earned twice.

Stop Immediately and Call for Help If

These signs could indicate a medical event. Do not wait to see if they pass.
  • New weakness or numbness on either side of the body
  • Sudden severe headache unlike any before
  • Slurred speech or trouble finding words
  • Loss of vision, double vision, or sudden blurring
  • Chest pain or pressure
  • Dizziness with loss of balance at rest
  • Sudden nausea or vomiting with no cause
  • Confusion or trouble understanding others
If any of these happen, call 911 immediately. Then call a family member. Do not drive yourself.
